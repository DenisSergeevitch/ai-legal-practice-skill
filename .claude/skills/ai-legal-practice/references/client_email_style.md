# Client email style guide (practical)

Goal: clients should understand risk and make decisions quickly.

## Voice

- Clear, direct, calm.
- Avoid legalese unless necessary.
- Prefer short paragraphs and bullets.
- Use concrete outcomes (“could allow termination without refund”) over abstract labels (“material breach risk”).

## Structure

1) **Subject**: specific, not vague
2) **Summary**: 1–3 bullets (BLUF)
3) **Key points**: grouped, severity-signposted
4) **Recommendations**: what you suggest doing and why
5) **What I need from you**: explicit questions / approvals
6) **Next steps / timing**: deadlines, who does what

## Risk framing

Use a simple scale:
- “Low risk”: mainly operational/clarity
- “Medium risk”: real downside but manageable or unlikely
- “High risk”: meaningful probability + meaningful downside

Tie risk to:
- money
- timing
- control
- IP/data
- ability to operate the business

## Negotiation framing

When appropriate, label items:
- “Worth fighting for”
- “Can concede”
- “Trade for X”

## Don’ts

- Don’t overstate certainty.
- Don’t bury the recommendation.
- Don’t send a wall of text.
