# Example prompts

These examples are written to trigger the skill’s playbooks.

## 1) Last-minute demand letter / markup triage

“Attached are:
- Purchase Agreement (fully executed draft)
- Disclosure Schedules
- Buyer’s demand letter proposing changes

We represent the seller. The deal must close tomorrow unless the economics are blown up. Please:
1) Extract each proposed change as a discrete item.
2) Map it to the affected sections/definitions and any schedules/side letters.
3) Flag contradictions with the disclosure schedules and internal conflicts created by the changes.
4) Provide severity ratings and negotiation-ready counter-positions with draft language.”

## 2) Vendor-side services agreement review

“Review this services agreement from the vendor’s perspective.
- Vendor has limited leverage and wants to close.
- Flag terms that shift risk beyond market.
- Check for missing provisions (LoL, IP, data handling, termination for convenience, etc.).
- Provide severity-rated issues and concrete counter-language.”

## 3) Create a redline package

“Apply the positions we chose:
- accept Sections 2.1 and 3.2 as-is
- revise limitation of liability to 12 months fees with confidentiality/IP carve-outs only
- narrow indemnity to third-party claims and add defense control language
- add a mutual confidentiality clause with standard exclusions

Deliver:
- Revised.docx
- Redline.html comparing Original vs Revised
- a cover email to opposing counsel, firm but professional”

## 4) Research memo with verification

“Prepare a research memo on [topic] for [jurisdiction].
- Use primary authority first.
- Verify every citation supports the proposition.
- Mark anything you cannot verify as UNVERIFIED.
- Provide BLUF + practical recommendations.”

## 5) Client email (decision-ready)

“Draft an email to the client explaining the top 5 issues we found, our recommended negotiation posture (fight vs concede), and the questions we need answered before we respond. Keep it clear and non-legalistic.”
